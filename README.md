# Tech Sector Equity Analysis: Alphabet, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft
### Comparative Stock Market Trend Analysis and Investment Potential

## Project Overview
This project provides a quantitative analysis of stock market performance for four major technology firms: Alphabet, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft. By leveraging historical trading data, the study explores volatility, price stability, and moving average trends to provide strategic investment insights.

## Key Objectives
* Statistical Analysis: Evaluate mean closing prices and daily return volatility.
* Trend Identification: Utilize 20-day and 60-day moving averages to identify bullish and bearish signals.
* Comparative Metrics: Benchmark Alphabet's performance against industry peers and the NASDAQ index.
* Predictive Modeling: Assess the correlation between trading volume and closing price movements.

## Technical Stack
* Python: Primary language for data manipulation and statistical computing.
* Pandas & NumPy: Used for data cleaning, time-series transformation, and metric calculation.
* Matplotlib & Seaborn: Employed for visualizing stock price fluctuations and moving average crossovers.
* Jupyter Notebook: Used for end-to-end development and documentation of the analysis.

## Analysis Methodology
1. Data Ingestion: Processing multi-company datasets from CSV and Excel sources.
2. Data Cleaning: Standardizing date formats and cleaning currency strings for numerical analysis.
3. Feature Engineering: Calculating rolling averages and standard deviations for risk assessment.
4. Qualitative Reporting: Synthesizing quantitative findings into a strategic investment report.

## Key Findings
* Stability Metrics: Analysis revealed a high degree of alignment between opening and closing prices for Alphabet, suggesting market stability during the period analyzed.
* Risk Assessment: Calculated daily return standard deviations to categorize stocks for short-term trading vs. long-term holding.
* Volume Trends: Identified robust trading volumes (averaging 30M+ for Alphabet) as a key indicator of continued investor interest.

## Repository Structure
* Code: Jupyter Notebook (.ipynb) and Python script documentation.
* Data: Historical datasets for Alphabet, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft.
* Documentation: Full strategic investment analysis report (DOCX/PDF).
